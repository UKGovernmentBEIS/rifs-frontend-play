Guidance on seminars

Objectives

The main rationale for these seminars is to help the universities and institutes we fund to team up with researchers and other professionals to promote best practice and our strategic priorities.

They are also a great opportunity to raise awareness of your own organisation’s strategies, policies and procedures, while safeguarding your intellectual assets.

Topics and speakers

The most successful events have involved senior academics working with colleagues to develop a research programme and share their strategic vision. There are no fixed rules about topics which can include intellectual asset management, licensing and collaborative .

A mix of activities such as presentations, case studies, audience participation and open discussion can help address issues, share experiences and communicate vision.

Feedback from previous events indicates that seminars lasting half a day are the most suitable.

Speakers might include internal or external business development professionals and others such as patent lawyers/agents and KTP advisors.

Whenever possible, a member of our Swindon office staff will be available to participate in the seminar free of charge.

Event audience

If possible, examine the audience make-up from previous similar events. Who came to them and who is likely to come to your event?

It’s a good idea to invite people from relevant faculties, colleges or departments, and business development offices.
